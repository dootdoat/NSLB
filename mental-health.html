Mental Health & Crisis Guide

College can be stressful. Mental health support is just as important as academic support. This guide provides:

  • A breakdown of OSU’s counseling and wellness services
  • What to expect in therapy and support groups
  • How to access emergency crisis help, both on and off-campus
  • Tips for maintaining mental well-being during midterms and finals

You’re not alone. OSU has help ready when you need it.

What services are available through OSU?

OSU offers multiple mental health and wellness options that are free or low-cost for students. The most commonly used services include:

  • Counseling and Consultation Service (CCS): Offers individual therapy, group therapy, crisis intervention, and drop-in hours. Location: 4th Floor, Younkin Success Center.
  • Student Wellness Center: Provides health coaching, stress relief classes, wellness assessments, and workshops. Location: RPAC.
  • Student Advocacy Center: Supports students facing crises with academic or personal issues.

24/7 Mental Health Support:

  • Call CCS: (614) 292-5766
  • National Text Line: Text “HELLO” to 741741
  • Suicide & Crisis Lifeline: Dial 988

These services are confidential and judgment-free. You don't need to be in a crisis to reach out—any level of concern is valid.

What should you expect in therapy or support groups?

Therapy at OSU starts with an initial phone screening to match you with the right resource. From there, you may be scheduled for:

  • Short-term individual counseling
  • Topic-based support groups (anxiety, identity, academics)
  • Workshops on stress management, test anxiety, or mindfulness

Support groups are peer-based and facilitated by professionals. They’re a safe space to connect with students facing similar challenges.

How to manage stress proactively

Stress isn’t always bad—but unmanaged stress can affect your sleep, focus, and overall well-being. Here are some tips to stay ahead of it:

  • Practice time management: Plan your week in advance and break large tasks into smaller pieces.
  • Get physical: Use the RPAC, go for walks, or try free fitness classes.
  • Use OSU’s free Headspace subscription for guided meditation and mindfulness.
  • Stay connected: Talk to a friend, join a club, or go to a campus event to avoid isolation.
  • Sleep matters: Try for 7–8 hours per night and avoid all-nighters.

If you’re feeling overwhelmed, talk to someone sooner rather than later. Help is always available—and it works.
