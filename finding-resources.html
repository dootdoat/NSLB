Finding Campus Resources

OSU has countless services available for students—you just need to know where to look. This guide will show you:

  • How to navigate the OSU website to find academic, mental health, and housing resources
  • Where to go for tutoring, writing help, and math support
  • How to connect with your college’s academic advising team
  • How to ask the right questions when visiting an office

We simplify the process so you can focus on getting the support you deserve.

Where do you begin your search?

Start with OSU’s main website (osu.edu) and click on “Current Students.” From there, you’ll find links to offices such as Academic Advising, the Student Wellness Center, the Younkin Success Center, and Buckeye Link. You can also use the search bar to directly look up specific help like “tutoring,” “mental health services,” or “academic coaching.”

Each academic college (e.g., College of Engineering, Arts and Sciences, Fisher College of Business) also maintains its own website with tailored resources, so check there too.

What resources are available?

Here are some of the top student support services at OSU:

  • Younkin Success Center: Offers academic coaching, tutoring, workshops on study skills, and career services.
  • Dennis Learning Center: Focuses on academic motivation, test preparation, and time management strategies.
  • Student Wellness Center: Provides health coaching, stress management, sexual health support, and financial wellness help.
  • Multicultural Center: A hub for cultural inclusion, peer groups, and diversity-focused events.
  • Buckeye Careers: Helps with resume writing, interview prep, internships, and networking.

Don’t forget other supports like Student Legal Services, Off-Campus Housing, and the Student Advocacy Center.

How should you approach staff at these offices?

Whether you’re visiting in person or reaching out by email, be clear and polite. You might say:

Hi, I’m a second-year student in the College of Arts and Sciences. I’m having trouble managing my study schedule this semester—could someone here help me find strategies or resources that could make it easier?

Staff members are there to help and will appreciate your honesty. If you’re unsure who to ask, they can often direct you to the right office or person.
