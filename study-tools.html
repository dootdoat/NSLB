Study Tools & Tips

Studying smarter, not harder, is the key. This article includes:

  • A list of free OSU-approved apps and tools for flashcards, scheduling, and note-taking
  • Tips on active recall, spaced repetition, and other proven study methods
  • Time management strategies tailored for OSU's semester system
  • How to set up an ideal study environment, even in a crowded dorm

With the right tools, you can take charge of your academic success.

Which apps and tools are worth using?

Here are some free and popular tools that OSU students swear by:

  • Notion: Great for organizing notes, syllabi, tasks, and study plans in one place.
  • Anki: Uses spaced repetition flashcards to help with memory-based subjects like biology, anatomy, and languages.
  • Google Calendar: Easily schedule classes, block out study hours, and set assignment reminders.
  • Grammarly: Helps polish your papers by catching grammar and tone issues.
  • Purdue OWL: A top resource for writing, formatting citations, and avoiding plagiarism.

OSU also gives access to Microsoft Office and Adobe tools through your student login—use them!

What study techniques actually work?

  • Active Recall: Instead of re-reading notes, quiz yourself from memory. This builds strong brain connections.
  • Spaced Repetition: Study material several times over increasing intervals (1 day, 3 days, 7 days) to lock in information.
  • Pomodoro Technique: Focus hard for 25 minutes, take a 5-minute break, and repeat. It helps you stay energized and avoid burnout.

Combining techniques gives the best results. Flashcards + active recall + short sessions = success.

How do you manage your time?

Time management starts with breaking large assignments into small steps and setting personal deadlines. Use Google Calendar or a planner to block study time each week. Reserve enough time for each class—aim for 2 hours of study per week for every credit hour.

Try this routine:

  • Sunday: Set weekly goals, organize your tasks, and prep materials
  • Monday–Friday: Stick to a consistent study schedule (e.g., 10am–12pm every day)
  • Saturday: Review the week and catch up

How do you make the most of your space?

Even in a dorm or shared apartment, you can create a productive environment:

  • Use noise-canceling headphones or ambient music (try Lo-Fi playlists)
  • Keep your desk tidy and limit distractions (put your phone out of reach)
  • Study in places like Thompson Library, the 18th Avenue Library, or quiet lounges on campus

Every student works differently, so test different methods and track what actually helps you learn better.
