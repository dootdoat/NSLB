Email & Communication Templates

Need to email a professor or ask for help, but not sure how? We've got you covered with plug-and-play templates:

  • How to ask for an extension respectfully
  • How to follow up if a professor hasn’t replied
  • How to request a letter of recommendation
  • How to write a professional email with confidence and clarity

Every student deserves to be heard—start here.

Why are good emails important?

Whether it’s a professor, TA, advisor, or staff member, how you communicate sets the tone. A clear, respectful email shows you’re serious, prepared, and professional. You don’t need to write a novel—just get to the point and stay polite.

General email tips:

  • Use your OSU email account
  • Always include a subject line
  • Begin with a greeting and end with your full name
  • Keep it short—3–5 sentences is usually enough

Sample Email: Asking for an Extension

Subject: Request for Extension on [Assignment Name]
Dear Professor [Last Name],

I hope this message finds you well. I’m writing to request a short extension on the [Assignment Name] originally due on [Date]. I’ve encountered [brief explanation — illness, family issue, workload overlap], and I’d really appreciate an extra [number] days to complete it thoroughly.

Thank you for your time and understanding.

Sincerely,
[Your Full Name]
[Course & Section]

Template: Following Up After No Reply

Subject: Follow-Up on Previous Email
Dear Dr. [Last Name],

I hope you’re doing well. I wanted to kindly follow up on the email I sent on [Date] regarding [topic]. I understand your schedule is very busy, but I’d appreciate any updates or guidance when possible.

Thank you again for your time.

Best,
[Your Full Name]

Template: Letter of Recommendation Request

Subject: Letter of Recommendation Request
Dear Professor [Last Name],

I hope all is well. I’m applying to [name of program/scholarship/job], and I’m wondering if you’d be willing to write a letter of recommendation for me. I’ve really valued your class and mentorship in [mention class or role], and I believe you could speak to [specific skills, qualities, or experience].

The deadline is [Date], and I’d be happy to send my resume or any additional details to help.

Thank you for considering it!

Sincerely,
[Your Full Name]

Template: Setting Up an Appointment

Subject: Request for Meeting
Dear [Advisor/Professor Last Name],

My name is [Your Name], and I’m a [year] majoring in [Your Major]. I’d like to set up a time to talk about [advising topic, academic concern, etc.]. Are you available this week during your office hours, or would another time work better?

Thank you in advance for your time.

Best regards,
[Your Full Name]
